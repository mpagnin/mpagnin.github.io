The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route

The GDMBR is a long distance cycle path from Banff, Alberta, Canada to Antelope Wells, New Mexico, USA.
It is a total of 2768 miles long that crisscrossing the Continental Divide north to south.
The entire route is basically dirt-road, mountain-pass, and jeep paths that make their way through forgotten passages of the Continental Divide.
Roughly 80% of it is composed of county, Forest Service (USFS), and Bureau of Land Management (BLM) dirt and gravel roads. The remainder is made up of four-wheel-drive tracks and singletrack trails, and paved roads.
